What's new in the box(es): summer recap edition

—by "I" in collaboration with EHC

The blurb

Welcome back sports fans, to the greatest sport known to gamblers and electronics thrifters alike: what I like to call tech-dumping. These past two weeks have been surprisingly active, with lots of cool stuff showing up in the tech dumps. A bunch of CRT monitors, VHS tapes, and other stuff I don't normally see. Read more about it below!

The summer recap

In any case, let's get a rundown of what happened over the summer. According to a source from the EHC...

The Department of Environmental Health and Safety (EHS) policed some of the big tech dumps (such as the JEC) over the summer, patrolling from 10 AM to 2 PM due to sensitive hard drive data being disposed of. Despite this, there were still items that were separated from patrol so that people could look through them. Eventually that stopped once the sensitive stuff was finally dumped properly.

As for actual things, there were a bunch of Apple PCs being dumped over the summer, as well as other general computer parts. Not much action overall until around 8/21, technically already past move-in for freshmen. Low received a whopping 7 boxes of extra tech dump stuff. There were mostly PCs there, unfortunately mostly gutted of parts. Before this even, there was also a mountain of things in the regular Low dump as well. Sounds like an interesting summer for sure, big thanks to the EHC member who let me know of all this!

Find of the week: boatload of tape media

In the DCC dump, as of around 9/4, there are boxes upon boxes of VHS, Betamax, TV broadcast tapes, Hi8, and other random formats for video. If you've heard of a format of video tape media, it's probably at the DCC! According to the EHC, there were a number of tapes containing, shall we say, spicy videos, but it looks like a bunch were also regular movies and TV recordings and stuff. There are even master tapes for broadcasting movies/TV! But there were also many unmarked tapes sitting there. Needless to say, I've never seen so many different tape formats, or just tapes in general, anywhere before. There's got to be at least 100 of 'em in there. Actually crazy stuff.

What's actually new in box(es)

MRC

The MRC has 3 laptops: a Thinkpad T43p, broken, an older Dell, and a 2010s-looking Lenovo, which is quite stripped down. Besides that, a few cassette tapes of what looks like music, as well as some data tapes which I've never heard of before.

Cogswell

Cogswell has at least 4 or 5 CRT monitors for the taking! 2 Trinitron CRTs, too. Not only that, but there's also a huge flatscreen TV (probably broken), and a few PC cases. Also on the outside, not sure if it's for grabs, just interesting, are a few chemistry journals from the mid-'80s.

DCC

Think of a type of tape media. That type of media was put next to the boxes in the DCC. VHS, Video8, MiniDV, Betamax, huge TV broadcast tapes, and probably everything in between. Really neat! Worth a gander for sure. The supplies of tapes have been added to over the past 2 weeks, their first appearance around the first day of classes.

Low

It's pretty empty now, especially compared to the 7-extra-box dump of PC parts around 8/21. Tuesday saw a big dump of LCD monitors in the right bin, they could still be there if you're in the market for one.

JEC

Disemboweled PCs, a flip clock, not much else.

VCC

Amazon Firestick found over the summer.

West

Lots of PC cases and cut Ethernet cables.

State of the Art: Bye Bye, Camera

You're here to see the art, I'm here to document the art, tunnel art is great. 'Nuff said. That being said, just wanted to report some news while I'm here. Over the summer, the DCC–JEC tunnels have been used as a storage place for heavy machinery, resulting in limited access. This may have been the reason that the camera was installed late last spring. Another reason, coming from an anonymous source in the spring, may have been due to the floor graffiti. It has not been confirmed the reason of the camera, but it is confirmed that you can rejoice that it's gone!

Plus, the DCC–JEC tunnels have been repainted near the start of this semester, leaving a blank canvas for the inevitable drawings to come. I'm excited to see what's going to unfold! Plus, the terrorists from the spring's Trolley Problem have left, as have the victims, safe and sound. In an interview with the victims, we asked if the lever was pulled or not. In other news, the lights in the DCC–JEC tunnel seem to be in the process of being replaced. It's too bad, the fluorescent lighting was less harsh than the LED lighting they're putting in.

Hope you enjoy the art on display this week! Some of the most intricate drawings I've personally seen so far, bravo to those who drew the big ones!

Tunnel Vision: Out of the Tunnel: Welcome back!

Welcome back or welcome to RPI! Hopefully you had a rejuvenating summer. Dear old RPI certainly did. For this first edition of the semester I wanted to catch you up on all things Rensselaer.

Games Room update

Although still technically under construction, the old Games Room has been given a major makeover. The first thing you'll notice is the all-glass paneling they added for a more open feel in the front. In the middle they've added a bunch more seating and tables. On the leftmost end there is a lounge titled the "Pre-Function Lounge." Presumably they will come up with a more specific function soon, but currently it features couches and smaller tables for a more relaxed vibe. It is through this lounge that you can access the new theater. On the opposite side, where Mother's used to be, they have pool tables and ping-pong tables, as well as more seating to watch people play. I think the most exciting addition to this space is the e-sports room. Complete with couches and large television screens, in addition to a huge table with at least eight monitors, this place is perfect for all sorts of gaming events.

Walkway under construction

The elevated walkway that spans the JEC is under construction, causing major delays as people try to navigate through the building instead. It's unclear how long the project will last, but given the pace and the ultimate goal of redoing that whole section, assume it'll be out of commission for the foreseeable future. To avoid getting stuck, I'd recommend going around the building completely.

This is one of multiple accounts doing this trend, and as far as we know, the administration hasn't caught wind of the alleged incidents.

Prospect Park

Prospect Park has undergone some fairly major renovations as well, including a new playground, updated tennis courts, and the addition of pickleball courts.

New Logo

But perhaps the most significant change facing RPI students this semester is the unveiling of our new logo. Sometime early to mid-August, President Marty Schmidt unveiled this new logo along with a media campaign to back this rebranding. The new logo features the letters RPI over a bar. The letters "RPI" are written in a custom weight of the Replica font and remain consistent across the many iterations of the logo. The bar however is where we see creative liberties being taken. This bar is supposedly a nod to the surveyor's rod featured in the center of the original seal and is the focal point of the new logo. The rebranding campaign features iterations of the logo with different types of bars representing everything from Delaunay triangulations to wavelengths. The administration is also encouraging student clubs to tailor the 500×36px space to represent their organizations. For inclusions to the official logo library, only black-and-white versions will be accepted.
