What's new in the box(es): Literally new boxes, that's what

—By I

Low Goes Crazy on Friday

Sometimes, I lose hope that anything interesting will show up somewhere like Low, when nothing new shows up there for weeks at times. Then I go to Low on an unsuspecting Friday and find 10 or even 11 boxes of things waiting to be looked through.

This week was pretty slow until Friday came through like a freight train. It appears that they emptied/replaced all the boxes (except for what seems like MRC/Cogswell/West's boxes) and just dumped 'em into Low. All the tech dump items you've seen for the past few weeks have found themselves in Low, in other words. Plus boxes that weren't before accessible, it seems.

A box from the VCC was spotted being hauled away to a truck outside around 2 PM on Friday. It had tons of really old Sun PCs and Dell Optiplexes (model GS, really old). Checking Low only a few hours before would have shown you nothing, checking around 5 or 6 would have shown you the literal motherload of stuff. Plus some new things that haven't been in the tech dumps before! If you're in the mood for an office photocopier, there are 2 in Low as of Friday. They're about 4 feet tall, and have a sign telling you to recycle them. There's probably a good chance they work, though I haven't tested them myself.

Besides that day, Tuesday also had some JEC finds, you can read about that and the less exciting finds below in What's Actually New.

Find of the Week: CRT TV/VCR/DVD Combo, Big PC Case, Old Sun PCs

I couldn't decide on just one find of the week, there was so much to choose from! The first find of the week this week was the CRT TV/VCR/DVD combo, since it worked perfectly, had vivid colors, and allowed us to see what was actually on some of the VHS tapes from the DCC. And to our surprise, it was not a bunch of spicy videos, it was actually a lot of old TV recordings! By the time I checked Low later on, the TV was already taken, but I'll never forget the precious time spent with it. The second find of the week was the biggest PC case I've ever seen in my life in Low, and the third multiple Sun PCs. I love the old "under-monitor" form factor the really old PCs have, and there were a lot of them.
