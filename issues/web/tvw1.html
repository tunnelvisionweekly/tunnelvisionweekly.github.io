What's new in the box(es)? Latest tech dump finds from the past week

Especially around this time of year, there doesn't seem to be very much in the tech dumps. Unless I go check at the wrong times, who knows? If you're anything like me, it can be very tempting to go check the tech dumps around campus, but sometimes, you just don't have the time to check every single one. Wouldn't it be nice to get a snapshot of the tech dumps without going there myself?

If you've ever asked yourself this, you're in the right place! In each edition of Tunnel Vision Weekly, I'll give my take on the new stuff in the tech dumps over the past week that isn't documented anywhere else! I'll cover the JEC, DCC, LOW, VCC, and sometimes Cogswell, MRC, and West Hall tech dumps. Occasionally stuff is posted in #tech-dump on the RPI free and for sale discord, but the info can be sparse there, to say the least. If you want to find maps of where to find the tech dumps, it's there, though.

What's new in the box(es)? (cont.)

I go to the tech dumps too much. It's like gambling, but free. The tech dumps here at RPI have lots of free stuff. All of it is free, actually. Except the box (don't ask me how I know :/). Sometimes perfectly working PCs; many times, the scavenged remnants of them. You never know what you're gonna get when you walk through those JEC doors, or down the hall of the DCC. It makes me sad to see perfectly good tech get thrown out, and I think if more people would know what's where, when, it may help rescue some tech from destruction, or salvage some neat trinkets from already broken stuff. It'd also be cool to see what you all may have found yourselves! Maybe you fixed up a neat gadget, or repurposed parts for a different thing? I wanna hear about it, and publish it here!

With that out of the way, onto the recent stuff I saw in the past week! It'll probably be easier to list it instead of show, but I'll include some pictures for the curious. There has been a little bit of an influx of new in the JEC dump.

A few days ago, in the JEC dump, I saw an old PC, 2 IDE hard drives intact, full of some DDR1 (I think) RAM, a floppy drive, and a CD drive. I came back later that day and it was gone, though. This could mean more PCs are on the way, though. I also found the remnants of a record player. It was pretty gutted, and gone the next day. There's some string lights and a heavy thing mover w/o the wheels (dolly?), too.

The DCC dump has been very quiet for quite a while, from what I've seen. Ever since those projectors were thrown out along with the barrage of wires, there's really been nothing of actual value, just broken metal casings and remnants of electronics left at this point. The industrial fan thing with a handle was cool, though!

LOW has had pretty much nothing new, but it usually doesn't, anyway. Bunch of nondescript garbage, basically. There's a halfway bent flatscreen TV in there, though. If you're looking for free glass shards, that's where you can look.

The VCC has a few good-condition-looking VGA monitors and a printer last time I checked, but besides that there isn't much.

TODAY, BREAKING NEWS: A flatscreen TV has been discovered in the MRC that appears to be fully working. It is uncertain if the picture-taker took the TV, but if you're in the market for one, check it out!

DIY Drunk Goggles from tech dump items! (cont.)

What is it?

Honestly, I don't know. There's this really magical plastic sheet that comes from monitors that I've found in the tech dumps recently. It's like this magnifying-looking sheet of plastic. It's got ridges and stuff, and if you've seen one of those page-size magnifiers, made of that thick, yet bendy plastic, it looks and feels like that. Only when you put it to your face, a pretty wild thing happens. Your entire vision shifts. It's like you are suddenly in the third person. You look down, and your legs are almost out of view, near the bottom of your vision! If you rotate the sheet, you can get other wild effects, such as doubled vision, and others.

I have no idea what this magic sheet of plastic is doing trapped inside a monitor, but I do know that it's really fun to mess around with. If you've ever messed around with drunk goggles in grade school, it's like that, but at like the strongest setting imaginable. Try to walk in a straight line with it, I dare you!

Where to find?

I found a sheet of this mysterious material in the DCC tech dump, it's got a footprint on it. That's probably the most specific I can be about it, besides the other stuff I said about it. In the picture below, you can see a closer look at where I last saw it. It's a mostly transparent-looking sheet with rainbow effects on it when you put it up to the light. You can find a picture of where I found it and an example perspective with it below.

What to do?

Like I said, all you gotta do is put it on your face. Maybe wash it first, but you can cut it out and stick it into some custom-made goggles, or just tape it to your face for maximum effect.

In conclusion, the next time you see a taken-apart monitor in a tech dump, take a second to look for this gem of a find!
