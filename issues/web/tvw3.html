What's new in the box(es): The Lab - Is it dumped?

I love CRT monitors. They're a relic of the past that most people have cast away in favor of newer, sleeker flatscreens. I mean, it makes sense. There's a lot to hate about CRTs, for one, their weight. Seriously, it feels like any decently sized CRT computer monitor weighs at least 50 lbs. Plus, they are boxy and quite difficult to fit on a lot of desks. They're thick. Quite thick. If you can look past that, though, I think there's a lot of stuff that has been taken away from us that once was commonplace with these monitors. For one, they are quite possibly indestructible. I heard a story of someone dropping one of them from a staircase, and it literally still worked. You can tap the screens with your finger without worrying about cracking the glass, too, that's for sure. Plus, you can technically set it to any resolution without issue (though some monitors get blurry at too high of a resolution), and you can actually get infinite color depth, due to the analog way that it displays things! I wish CRTs were still in use in some capacity, but alas, it was not meant to be, I guess. If you have the space and arm strength to get yourself a CRT monitor or TV, I'd recommend it!
