What's new in the box(es): Apple edition

Just when you least expect it

It's weeks like these that keep me going to the tech dumps, honestly. There I was, existing, right after the FOCS exam this past Wednesday. I was pretty tired, but I still decided to go on a little tech dump check. When I got to the VCC, I wasn't expecting much. Only when I got down there, I was treated to a plethora of iPads (10 or so), all freshly reset. And all of them just worked (except for 2 activation-locked ones), it was absolutely bonkers, as the cover says. In addition, on Monday, the EHC notified others of the dumping of at least 10 PCs, also in the VCC, all of which presumably had everything intact, besides the HDDs!

Find of the week: (8) Apple iPads

iPads are heavy, or at least the iPad 4 is. You'd be surprised how heavy 10 of them are stacked on top of one another, but I digress. If you've been quick enough to snag an iPad for yourself, you may be wondering what you can do with it, seeing as it's so old. It's locked to iOS 10!

First, a quick disclaimer

This is an old device—the batteries work fine, but they're over a decade old at this point, so I'd be at least a little more careful than usual with the batteries. Also, since it is iOS 10, it's probably not as secure as the most recent iOS, so just keep that in mind. Still fun to mess around with, though!

What to do

The first issue I found with these was they wouldn't connect to the school Wi-Fi. Supposedly they can. I couldn't, so I used a hotspot on my phone to connect them to the Internet. Once I got that set, I was able to create an Apple ID, connect it to the iPad, and open iTunes and the App Store and all that. iTunes seems to work perfectly fine, but the App Store is a bit more of a struggle. If you want to install apps to this thing, you're going to have to do a slight workaround. What I had to do was install version 12.6 or earlier of iTunes on my PC, access the App Store through that, "get" the apps via that PC App Store, and then use the App Store on the iPad to install an earlier, more compatible version of the app via the "purchased" tab. Not all apps will work, but if you want to do some classic iOS gaming, this method should work well for most classic apps, especially those made before 2017/the release of i0S 11!

As a side note, YouTube works pretty solidly on these iPads as well. There isn't even lag (from my testing)!

Tunnel Vision: Out of the Tunnel

Student review of Ricky Montgomery Concert
—by anonymous

On Saturday, April 5th, Ricky Montgomery and Sean Solomon came to dear old RPI thanks to the efforts of UPAC Concerts, the student-run group responsible for bringing musicians to campus (check them out on Instagram @upacconcerts). If you, like the majority of students, didn't attend last week's show, allow me to fill you in on what you missed.

Highlights included the live debuts of multiple songs, including unreleased tracks from both Sean and Ricky. Rather than using the multimillion dollar Experimental Media and Performing Arts Center (EMPAC), a stage was erected in the ECAV gymnasium, which both Ricky and his opener made note of, as it apparently reminded them of being bullied in high school. The turnout was nothing spectacular, a crowd of under 150 RPI students as well as some hardcore fans who just really wanted to see Ricky. The woman in front of me filmed a single continuous video of the entire hour-and-a-half performance, just to give you an idea. One of the non-RPI affiliates brought a sign reading "I don't even go here I just love you Ricky".

The combination of passionate fans and low crowd turnout made for a very intimate experience in the end. The opener, Sean Solomon, played his new single "Car Crash" and a number of songs that are not yet available to stream. He engaged with the crowd, telling the stories behind his songs and even having a few back-and-forth exchanges with audience members. Armed only with a guitar and a mini TV, each of his songs had an accompanying animation created by Sean himself. Fans of Ricky Montgomery, Jack Stauber, and cut//slack might want to check him out on Spotify or Apple Music.

After Sean left the stage there were about 20 to 30 minutes of downtime before Ricky came out. During which time, speakers played pop songs that had been edited to include a robotic voice saying "Rick". "I can make the bad guys -Rick- for a weekend" ("Blank Space" by Taylor Swift). Around 9:05 PM Ricky took the stage, opening with "Line Without a Hook." His performance did not disappoint. Speaking as someone who is only mildly familiar with his discography, and only knew the words to a few of the 17 total songs (full set list below), he is a great performer. He engaged with the crowd, even taking recommendations and changing the set list accordingly. He made sure to include favorites like "This December" and "Mr. Loverman" as well as some songs by The Honeysticks.

Some fan highlights included "Last Night"—a highly requested song on his last tour, but he never played it because he was "scared"—and the live debut of "Penny Come Home." The second live debut was of "Change," an unreleased song he decided last-minute to play for us. Once he finished he brought out copies of the set list that had been folded into paper airplanes and threw them into the crowd, a fun and (in my opinion) dangerous way for a handful of people to fight to take home a special souvenir of the night. That is, if you're a chump. The friend I attended the concert with (they're a big Ricky fan) went up to one of the crew members and asked if they could have a guitar pick that had ended up on the floor, to which the crew member happily obliged. Work smarter, not harder. Overall the concert was a 9/10, more than worth the 25$ ticket fee. Thank you, Mr. Montgomery!

Want to hear an unreleased track of his? Head on over to tinyurl.com/tvw-new!
